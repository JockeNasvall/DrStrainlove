Guidelines for using the DA strain collection database (Dr. Strainlove)

Remember that the descriptions of the strains in the strain collection should be understandable by anybody in the lab, without access to your lab notes. If you give a strain a temporary genotype while waiting for sequencing results etc. remember to update it ASAP. Please do not enter a strain that does not yet exist into the database. If you really need to reserve strain numbers before the strains are ready to be frozen, leave a clear indication in the comment field that the strain is not frozen yet.

Genotype:

Should correctly identify the species, strain, isolate etc. and contain a list of all mutations it has compared to the wild-type. In cases when the mutation causes a defined phenotype, this may be indicated in the description of the mutation e.g. rpoB(S531L, RifR), etc. Evolved strains should be indicated by “Evolved” in the genotype.

Please keep other information (primer sequences, references, who you got the strain from etc.) to the comment field. Avoid putting commas or full stops in the genotype, and don’t put spaces in gene designations. It makes the genotype open for misunderstanding. rpoB(S531L) is correct, rpoB (S531L) is not. If you notice a mistake in a genotype of your own strain, fix it. If you notice a problem in the genotype of some other persons strain, ask the person responsible for the strain to check it out and fix it if necessary.

Always start with an indication of which species the strain is. Some examples of acceptable abbreviations and their meaning:

Sty

Salmonella enterica subsp. enterica serovar Typhimurium (strain LT2 if not indicated otherwise)

Eco

Escherichia coli (K12 strain MG1655 if not indicated otherwise)

Kpn

Klebsiella pneumonia

Vna

Vibrio natriegens

Spy

Streptococcus pyogenes

Sau

Staphylococcus aureus

For all other organisms than E. coli K12 MG1655 and S. Typhimurium LT2, the species designation should always be followed by an indication of which strain it is or which ATCC number it has. For strains from other groups, put the original strain number in the genotype, e.g.:

Sty 14028 or Sty ATCC14028

Other strain than LT2

Eco K12 W3110 BW25113

Strain from other group (BW=Barry Wanner)

Vna ATCC14048

ATCC number

Sty TH194

Strain from other group (TH= Diarmaid Hughes’ Salmonella collection)

Use as correct genetic nomenclature as possible. See Maloy and Hughes (2007) and Demerec et al (1966) for some detailed guidelines. Keep the same designation for the same mutation in every strain it’s in. We don’t assign allele numbers for mutations that we generate, but if a mutation already has an allele number, keep it. When you have found or constructed a mutant, make sure that the way you type it in is unique in our database. If you e.g. construct a new hisA deletion that’s not identical to the one that’s already in the database (DELhisA), you have to give a unique identifier to your mutant (e.g. DELhisA2).

Examples for how to write specific mutations:

Deletion of one gene:

DELhisA

Deletion of a region containing more than one gene:

DELaraBAD, DEL(flhE-flhD)

Insertion in a gene:

miaB::Tn10dTet

Replacement (= deletion plus insertion) of a gene:

DELgalE::Tlux, DELhisA::kan

Replacement of a region:

DEL(hisOGDCBHAFI)::kan, DEL(hisO-hisI)::kan, DEL(araA-thiP)::cat

Rearrangements should ideally have an indication of where both junctions are:

Duplication:

DUP(rrnB-rrnE)

Inversion:

INV(tufB-tufA)

Rearrangement with a selection marker at the junction:

DUP(genE)*amp*(genX)

Parents:

The parental strain is always written in the recipient field. The donor field is restricted to the source of any DNA that has been transferred into the parental strain by transformation, transduction or conjugation. A strain that has been constructed by direct selection for a spontaneous mutation or in an evolution experiment thus only has a single parental strain (recipient), it has no donor strain as no DNA has been transferred.

Tips for searches:

Wildcards:

%

Any string of characters. his% finds everything starting with his. ara%kan finds everything containing first ara and then kan separated by any number of characters.

_

Any single character. his_::kan finds everything starting with his separated by exactly one character from ::kan (e.g. hisA::kan, hisF::kan and hisB::kan). hisA::_a_ will find e.g. hisA::kan, hisA::cat and hisA::iAcatsac
